**Part A — Problem Framing**
Business Objective To solve the systemic availability crisis across the 18-store retail network. By shifting from a reactive "empty shelf" ordering model to a data-driven Statistical Reorder Point (ROP) policy, the project aims to minimize lost sales while preventing overstocking of low-demand items.

**North Star Metric**
Total Revenue Recovery (₹): The projected financial gain from fulfilling "True Demand" that is currently lost due to stockouts.

Stockout Rate Reduction: Moving the current 99.2% stockout rate toward a 95% Service Level target.

Supporting KPIs

Service Level (95%): The probability of having an item in stock during a lead time cycle.

Average Daily Demand: The 28-day smoothed sales rate per SKU/Store.

Safety Stock (Buffer): The extra units held to protect against demand volatility.

Days of Cover: on_hand_units / avg_daily_demand (identifying how long current stock will last).

Reorder Point (ROP): The specific inventory level that triggers a new Purchase Order.

Recommended Order Quantity: The optimized batch size to return stock to target levels.

**Part B — Data Architecture & ETL**
Data Pipeline (etl_pipeline.py)

Ingestion: Processes 6 raw inputs (stores, products, inventory, sales, pos, calendar).

Standardization: Cleans headers, removes duplicates, and unifies date formats.

Feature Engineering: * Calculates stockout_flag where on_hand_units == 0.

Generates avg_4w_demand using a 28-day rolling window.

Computes demand_std_dev to measure sales risk.

Output Generation: Produces the curated files used for the Power BI Dashboard.


**Part C — Analytics (Insights + KPIs)**
Key Findings (analysis.ipynb)

The Empty Store Paradox: Analysis revealed that 99% of SKUs are currently in a stockout state during the measurement period.

Daily Loss: The network is losing approximately ₹217,761 every day in potential revenue.

Category Winners: Grocery and Snacks show the highest "Revenue Saved" potential, making them the priority for the new replenishment rollout.

**Part D — Replenishment PolicyThe Logic**

 (final_replenishment_policy.csv)Safety Stock: Calculated as $Z (1.645) * Demand Std Dev * (Sq.root of Lead Time)

 Reorder Point: The sum of Lead Time Demand and Safety Stock.

 Target Stock Level: Designed to cover 14 days of demand plus the safety buffer.
